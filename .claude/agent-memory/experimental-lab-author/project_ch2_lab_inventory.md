Chapter 2 has the following experimental labs as of 2026-05-24:

**Delivered:**
- **E2.1 `faraday-generator` — Faraday's Law in a Rotating Coil** (software, PhET Generator). Three controlled experiments: ε vs ω, ε vs N, ε vs B. Student computes theoretical peak EMF from ε = NBAω before measuring, then compares to PhET's voltmeter. Back-calculates NBA product from slope. Runtime 60–90 min. Difficulty: core.

**Style observations:**
- E2.1 uses a "theory first, then measurement" pedagogical structure. Each data table has columns for both the theoretical prediction (computed from Faraday's law) and the PhET measured value. Worked examples fill the first two rows of each table.
- The lab explicitly tells students that PhET readings may differ by ~10% from theory due to simulation specifics; what matters is the proportionality and the constant ratio.
